# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Patients undergoing physical therapy, primarily using the app on their phones. They're often recovering from injury or managing a chronic condition — motivated but easily bored by repetitive exercise routines. They use the app in short daily sessions, alone, at home or in recovery.

## Product Purpose

MedProj turns physical therapy into something patients *want* to do. Patients perform PT exercises whose movements mirror planting and tending gestures — raising arms, rotating wrists, reaching — guided in real time by an AI that reads their posture via the front camera. Each completed session earns stars and grows a virtual tree. When a milestone is reached, a partner NGO plants a real tree in the patient's name.

The app has two inseparable parts:
1. **AI-guided exercise sessions** — the front camera detects the patient's posture and provides movement guidance so they practice correctly and safely, without a human therapist present.
2. **Progress + tree tracking** — daily stars, completion streaks, and a living tree that grows as the patient does.

Success looks like: patients completing their prescribed PT, reducing dropout, and feeling that their effort had meaning beyond themselves.

## Brand Personality

Gentle, grounded, alive. The tone is like a calm friend who tends a garden — encouraging without being cheerful for the sake of it, purposeful without being clinical.

Three words: **nurturing, purposeful, alive**

Emotional goal: replace the dread of repetitive therapy with the quiet satisfaction of watching something grow because of your effort.

## Anti-references

- **Too cute / consumer-toy**: no emoji overload, no cartoon mascots, no bouncy micro-interactions. The nature metaphor is serious and meaningful, not decorative.
- **Legacy clinical UI**: no cold blues, no form-heavy screens, no hospital-portal aesthetic.
- **Aggressive gamification**: no urgent streaks that shame patients for missing a day. Duolingo's pressure loop is the wrong model.

Reference feel: **Forest app + Calm** — peaceful, nature-forward, gentle. The interface recedes; the patient's progress and their tree are the focal point.

## Design Principles

1. **Meaningful over mechanical** — every UI element should connect the patient's movement to something that grows. The exercise is never just a rep count.
2. **Nature as the visual language** — seasons, growth stages, soil, light. Organic shapes and earthy palette. Not as decoration — as the structural metaphor.
3. **Gentle accountability** — progress is shown with warmth, not pressure. Missing a day makes the tree pause, not die.
4. **AI as silent guide** — the posture AI should feel like a calm, knowledgeable presence, not a robotic overlay. Guidance is minimal, clear, and kind.
5. **Mobile-first ritual** — designed for a 5–15 minute daily practice. Every screen should feel like the right thing to do right now, not a task to get through.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (sensible default for a healthcare-adjacent app).
Key considerations:
- Patients may have limited mobility — touch targets must be generous (≥48px), no small gestures.
- Camera-based posture detection must degrade gracefully if camera is unavailable or denied.
- Reduced motion support required — some patients may have vestibular sensitivities.
- Color must not be the sole indicator of progress or feedback (use shape + label alongside color).
